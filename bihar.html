1. Alu-Chokha (Spicy Potato)

Ingredients:

• 2-3 potatoes (medium cubed)
• 1big onion (chopped)
• 3-4 cloves garlic (chopped)
• 1 teaspoon amchur (dried mango powder)
• 1 teaspoon whole coriander seeds
• 2-3 dried red chili
• 3-4 green chilli
• 1 teaspoon cumin powder
• 1 teaspoon coriander powder
• 1/2 cup of chopped coriander leaves
• 1/2 cup of mustard oil
• 3/4 cup of water
• Salt according to taste

Preparation:

1. Heat the oil in a pan and saute the dried red chili, coriander seeds.
2. Add chopped onion, green chili and chopped garlic.
3. When the onions turn red add the potatoes and fry for 3-4 minutes.
4. Now add salt, cumin-coriander powder and 3/4 cup of water.
5. Cook it for 10 min in a medium flame with cover.
6. When the gravy becomes dry, place it down.
7. Sprinkle amchur powder and mix it with the potatoes.
8. Sprinkle the chopped coriander laeves.
9. Enjoy hot Alu-chokha.

2. Chana dal Khichdi

Ingredients:

• 2 cups of rice (washed)
• 1 cup of chana dal
• 2 Cardamoms
• 2 Cloves
• 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
• 1 teaspoon whole cumin seeds
• 1 bay leaf
• 1-2 star anise
• 2 small onions (sliced)
• 4 - 6 green chilies (chopped)
• 1 teaspoon ginger paste
• 1 teaspoon garlic paste
• 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
• 3-4 tablespoon mustard oil
• 1 tablespoon sugar
• Salt to taste
• Coriander leaves for garnishing (optional)

Preparation:

1. Wash the rice and soak in water for 15-20 min.
2. Wash the dal and half cooked it.
3. Heat the oil in a pan, sauté the cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, cumin seeds,bay leaf and star anise.
4. Add onion and sauté until translucent. Add green chilies and ginger garlic and sautéuntil rawness of the ginger is gone.
5. Add rice and pre-cooked chana dal, turmeric, sugar, salt according to taste and cook for a minute.
6. Add 3 cups of water, stir it properly and cook it for 15-20 min in a medium flame till it becomesdry and tender.
7. When the Khichdi is ready to serve, sprinkle the coriander leaves on top.

3. Mitha Khaja (dessert)

Ingredients:

• 2 cups of white flour
• 1 cup of jaggery
• 1 cup water
• 1 teaspoon cardamom powder
• Ghee (for deep frying)

Preparation:

1. Heat the water and soaked the jaggery, till it dissolves in water.
2. Strain the jaggery water and cool.
3. Mix the cardamom powder and ghee with the flour, knead the flour with the jaggery water.
4. The dough should be stiff but pliable.
5. Divide the dough in approx. 20 parts.
6. Knead each with palm and roll in 4" round discs.
7. Make many tiny slits with knife or fork on both sides.
8. Keep them aside on a clean cloth for an hour to make it dry.
9. Deep fry in hot ghee on shimmering flame, till it becomes light golden & keep aside.
10. The khajas will become crisper and harder as they cool.
11. Store in airtight container after cooling completely.

4. Chaawal ki Kheer

Ingredients:

 • 5 cups full cream milk
• 1/4 cup parsai ke chaawal-washed
• 1/2 cup sugar
• 10-12 raisins
• 4 green cardamoms
• 10-12 almonds-blanched, shredded

Preparation:

 • Put rice and milk in a deep pan and bring to a boil.
• Simmer over low flame, stirring occasionally till the rice is cooked and the milk has thickened. • This should take about 25 minutes.
• When cooked, add sugar, raisins and cardamoms.
• Stir till sugar is dissolved and 10-15 minutes after that.
• Transfer onto a serving dish, garnish with almonds.
• Serve hot or chilled.
